Dynamo in Migration from a Desktop Tool to Support Autodesk Cloud

Dynamo is a 11,000+ MAU automation tool in Autodesk's AEC (Architecture, Engineering & Construction) software collection. Within the Computational and Design Automation group, Dynamo supports Desktop-based leading 3D modeling software --Revit, Civil3D, Alias, and FormIt.

As Autodesk pivoted on its cloud platform strategic reorganization in 2021, the Dynamo team collaborate with Autodesk Platform Service to move ahead to a platoform-based service. Dynamo is heading towards an exciting stage by the new opportunities brought by the cloud to push the efficiency frontier forward through batch-data selection for automation and its intuitive user experience . The 12-week project focus on researching, designing, and prototyping new Dynamo Nodes for data selection for cloud.

User Interview

Understand Dynamo's Use Cases and Unique Value through Interview

Recruitment -- Diversity in Community

With an established user group and its community via Dyanmo Forum and Dynamo Linkedin Groups, I recruited a pool of interviewees and selected five of them with diversity in experience, Dynamo environment, and working environment. The diversity of interviewees draws a user persona in each of them.
Three of them have above five years of experience with Dynamo, one is intermediate level and one beginner level. Three of them use Dynamo in Revit, one of them using Dynamo in Civil3D, and one use Dynamo Sandbox for testing plugins. They are from different working environment, covering BIM consultant companies, in-house BIM technician in architecture firm, and architectural design agencies.

Method -- Interview, Qualitative and Quantitative analysis

The user research method includes interviews conducted through Zoom and qualitative and quantitative analysis via Dovetail. Dovetail enables direct zoom recording integration and generates accurate and readable transcripts in paragraphs. With organized videos in pipeline, I went back to those recordings and transcripts with quanlitative analysis -- taging content and highlighting storng quotes. With Dovetail integrated tools, the quantitative analysis cross-interviews organized tags into graphs, created tables for similar patterns in cases, and generated word cloud. Through the word graph, we see Dynamo is an automation tool for productivity, with is strength in computational and generative design and management in data workflow.

An Example for Dynamo Cloud Connection's Benefit

-- Calculate Door Fire Rating in Models to Meet Building Codes

In F.B.'s case, he would like to check Revit Data sheet with regional codes to make sure all doors meet the building fire codes. Here we compare three workflow with Revit, Dynamo, and Dynamo Cloud connection.

    [Revit] -- F.B. compared data cross-platform. He had Revit Schedule to view data from the 3D design file, and compared it with the imported building codes in Excel.   manual work low accuracy
    [Dynamo] -- The comparison is automated by in Dynamo where it reads data from Excel and compares within platform to check 3D model data.   automation high accuracy
    [Dynamo Cloud Connection] -- With Autodesk Cloud connected, multiple design files can be measured with a single click. Especially with projects in the same region where they share the same building codes. For fire code inspector, they can even reduce manual work to open, close, and run files through the Dynamo Batch automation.   automation high accuracy batch files

Collaboration & Community

Cross-Organization Collaboration

Having been a part of a large tech firm, I deeply valued was the abundance of resources and a pool of exceptional talents in the organization. As Dynamo functions as a cross-product and cross-platform automation tool, fostering seamless communication and collaboration across various teams becomes indispensable.

Throughout the course of my internship, we proactively engaged with platform service teams, working hand-in-hand to seamlessly integrate their web-based interfaces such as the Large Model Viewer and AEC Data Model into Dynamo. This collaboration aimed at delivering an enriched and immersive user experience. As the discussions unfolded and the teams ventured into exploration, I identified and suggested pertinent shortcuts based on insights garnered from lessons learned by other groups. This input notably contributed to the efficient reallocation of resources within the team.
