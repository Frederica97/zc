Problem

Rroproductive Health in Teenage/College Women+ Requires Attention

Over 50% of young women+ in college have reported experiencing reproductive health conditions or issues. These individuals often experience feelings of depression, anxiety, and isolation because they are unable to openly discuss their diagnoses in a society that stigmatizes such conditions. Even when efforts have been made to curb these issues, the current solutions have failed to provide women with a secure, trusting, and organized space to come together.

Solution

Destigmatizing the Discussion of Women+'s Reproductive

Lotus supports conversations about reproductive health conditions and infertility diagnoses among women of all age groups, with a special focus on young women+ with limited access to resources. Our primary objective is to establish a platform where women can come together, build connections, engage in social interactions, and openly discuss topics related to reproductive health and infertility.

Value Proposition

A Safe and Secured Platform

Promoting online discourse about reproductive health, we will tackle hate speech and inappropriate language. Lotus's 24/7 algorithm-based moderator is dedicated to content curation, ensuring a secure space for women+ to engage. In addition to safety measures, we've developed various community connection options, including match-to-chat, group forums, and one-on-one chats.

Secured

A fine-tuned AI model moderates content 24/7, filters hate speech and inappropriate language on topics about reproductive health.

Group Forum

A topic-based supportive, informative, and inclusive community to share information, break stigma, and provide emotional support.

Individual Chat

Establish real, meaningful connections in an intimate environment, from reproductiion related topics to life.

Algorithic Matching

Facilitate individual connections by swiping to engage in meaningful conversations about reproductive health.

Design in Phases

While we have a clear vision for community engagement, we remain adaptable to market-driven changes. During the MVP and Beta stages, the development team establishes feature milestones. Each design is provisional yet thorough, prioritizing user experience for each testing stage. Simultaneously, user interviews inform our task pipeline, aligning with our product vision and user feedback.

User Interview

Usability Test for MVP

Lotus values every opportunity to engage with both existing and potential customers. We conducted four usability tests for MVP. Each interview consisted of establishing a personal connection with Lotus, conducting usability test, and discussing upcoming features that users are anticipating.

These conversations have yielded invaluable insights into understanding users' motivations for participating in our community. The usability tests have provided essential feedback on user flow and user interface design, where we made adjustment in the Beta version. Through careful observation and dialogue, we've recognized a recurring pattern where users are seeking more from our platform. This realization has prompted us to begin outlining our next features. Please stay tuned, more to come from Lotus!

Thoughts

What did we learn?

Design is a journey defined by its iterative nature, marked by a perpetual loop of creation, assessment, and enhancement. This process extends beyond the mere crafting of visually appealing interfaces or functional systems; it represents a profound learning adventure that unfolds as we engage users and the message Lotus conveys. Within this ongoing cycle of improvement, it's imperative to acknowledge the juncture where we pause and evaluate the progress, to leverage Lotus's vision and the market.
